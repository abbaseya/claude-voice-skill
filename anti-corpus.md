```
A few thoughts on why building reliable AI systems matters more than ever.

In today's fast-paced digital landscape, organizations are increasingly relying on AI to drive transformation. Here's what I've learned: the reality is that most AI deployments fail not because of model quality, but because of organizational alignment. Furthermore, the truth is that without proper governance, even the best models will underperform.

Let me know my thoughts in the comments!
```

Diagnosis:
- "A few thoughts on" / "Here's what I've learned" / "Let me know my thoughts" are AI-default openers and closers. They announce the writing instead of just being the writing.
- Buzzwords stacked: "fast-paced digital landscape", "drive transformation", "organizational alignment". None of these would appear in my real writing.
- "Furthermore" / "The truth is" / "The reality is" are formulaic transition markers. I'd use a period and a new sentence.

```
# What I learned from launching my latest product

Output is up. Significantly. I'm shipping what used to take weeks in just a few days — end-to-end SDKs, planning through implementation through review. The amount of work I can move through has gone up by something like 5×. The calendar time to ship is down by 80% or more. That part is real and not going away.

## What's working
[...]

## What nobody warned me about
[...]
```

Diagnosis:
- `##` section headings used as visual scaffolding before the prose has earned them. I structure pieces with story-arc prose, not Markdown headings.
- "Output is up. Significantly. I'm shipping..." restates the same fact three ways in three sentences. I pick one phrasing and stick with it.
- "# What I learned from launching..." is a Medium/Substack title style I don't reach for; my titles are shorter and topic-named.

```
This new approach is not just better — it's transformative. It's not a minor improvement. It's the kind of leap that changes everything.

The numbers are not a typo: 10× faster, 80% less cost, zero downtime. Read that again.
```

Diagnosis:
- "Not just X — it's Y" / "It's not minor. It's the kind of leap that changes everything." are dramatic-one-liner stacks. I'd use one short emphatic sentence at most, not three in a row.
- "Not a typo" / "Read that again" are AI emphasis markers — I'd write "yes, I read that correctly" if I needed to draw attention to a number, but usually I'd just trust the number to speak for itself.
